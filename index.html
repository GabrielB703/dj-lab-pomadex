01 — About

Beginners to professional

An authentic project, built for the craft

This is a private and exclusive work, born in Dublin with the purpose of offering a deep, authentic experience for new DJs and music enthusiasts who want to develop a true art of disc-jockeying and musical research — exploring sound, style, and cultural context through an artistic, contemporary lens.

Technique, perception, artistic presence

From the needle on vinyl to the digital controller, the DJ is the link between artist and audience — shaping atmosphere and leading the dance floor. Learning to DJ is learning to listen, feel, and express: uniting technique and creativity. The goal is to train complete DJs — artists who understand music, master sound, and comprehend the cultural role of the dance floor.

Purpose and mission

More than a course, this is an artistic DJing laboratory — a space to learn, experiment, and perfect the act of mixing, whether on vinyl or digital controllers. Each class is customized to the student's level, style, and goals: theoretical and practical, or practical only, always exploring the balance between technique, expression, and sound research.

Disc-jockeying is the art of controlling the sound, the time, and the emotion.

04 — Curriculum

What you'll learn

Six areas that combine technique, perception, creativity, and performance — through to actually getting booked. You set the focus, the curriculum bends to what you want to work on.

01

DJ culture & concept

  • History and evolution of turntablism
  • The DJ's role in musical culture
  • Genres, styles, and floor reading
  • Curation and sonic identity
02

Gear, formats & setup

  • CDJs, mixers, turntables, controllers, headphones
  • Assembling and connecting a professional rig
  • Vinyl, CDs, USB, and digital platforms (MP3, WAV, AIFF, FLAC)
  • Signal flow and library management in Rekordbox
03

Music theory & sound

  • Tempo, BPM, and phrasing
  • Harmonic mixing and key relationships
  • Track structure and arrangement
  • Reading energy and dynamics in a track
04

Mixing & performance

  • Manual beatmatching and cueing
  • EQ, filters, and creative effects
  • Transitions, cuts, and hybrid mixing
  • Energy control and crowd reading
05

Identity & artistic growth

  • Guided, supervised free practice
  • Developing your sonic identity
  • Stage presence and artistic performance
  • Recording and analysis of your sets
06

Bookings & professional practice

  • Building a promo mix and demo reel
  • Approaching promoters, clubs, and festivals
  • Booking etiquette and professional communication
  • Growing your online presence

The curriculum is modular — modules adapt to your pace.

05 — Tips

Sharpen your practice

Simple habits that make the difference between a student who takes classes and a DJ who's actually building a craft.

01
Bring your own music
Pen drive, vinyl, or a playlist — come with tracks you actually want to mix.
02
Record your practice
Record your sessions and listen back — you'll catch things your ears miss live.
03
Practice between classes
Fifteen minutes a day already makes a real difference over a few weeks.
04
Watch other DJs
Observe sets, analyze styles and techniques, and note what moves a room.
05
Explore new ground
Try new BPMs and genres outside your comfort zone — it sharpens your ear.
06
Study the floor
Learn track structure and how a dance floor actually behaves and responds.
07
Organize by energy
Build playlists around energy and dance floor moment, not just genre.
08
Trust your ear
Develop active listening — trust your ear more than the display.
09
Work on your identity
Your image and artistic identity are part of the craft, not an afterthought.
10
Stay consistent
Consistency, focus, and passion — that's what actually builds a real DJ.
